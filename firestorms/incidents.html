British Columbia Firestorm

In British Columbia, August 12, 2017, a massive wildfire outbreak occurred. The massive wildfire had created it's own storm system, being classified as a "pyroCbs," or a Firestorm.

In the end the firestorm consumed 4 700 square miles of British Columbia. Five total Firestorms had formed, the resulting smoke being detected almost as far as the North Pole. This was named the "Mother of all pyroCbs," dealing over 127 million dollars in damage.

Chimney Tops 2 Fire

A wildfire fighting in progress on November 23, 2016, a few days after the wildfire evolved. Humidity dropped and strong winds moved over in the period of a day. The massive plume of smoke and winds stopped helicopters from engaging. Winds from 40-50mph with gusts of 87mph flooded the area.

No structures in the park were damaged directly, but the wind did do minor damage. 17 000 acres were burned.

Spokane Firestorm

In 1991, a massive firestorm rocked Spokane. After forty-two days without rain or much weather of any kind, 50 mph winds rushed into the area. Later, smoke from a nearby fire invaded the weather office.

When the day ended 80 wildfires had ravaged the land. 112 homes were destroyed, two people were killed, and 50 000 acres were scorched. Some of the fires still burned out of control.
